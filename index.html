Interpretive Summary

Water-mediated percolation functions as a hydrophobicity-dependent sorting mechanism. Target compounds (cannabinoids, log P ≥ 7) occupy the maximally hydrophobic end of the spectrum and pass through with minimal loss (~5%). Harmful combustion byproducts—aldehydes, HCN, phenols—are polar and water-soluble, yielding interaction rates of 85–92%.

The principal cost is a ~25% attenuation of terpene content (the “selectivity tax”), driven by their intermediate polarity. The system's blind spot is carbon monoxide, which passes through at ~96% due to its exceptionally low aqueous solubility—the one significant toxin this filtration method does not address.

Practical bottom line: The system removes what hurts you, keeps what you want, slightly dulls what you taste, and completely misses CO. For harm reduction, it's remarkably well-aligned by chemistry alone — the one gap requires ventilation, not better filtration.

Note: Interaction rates are approximate estimates derived from physical chemistry principles (Henry's Law constants, log P values, condensation thermodynamics) and available empirical literature. Values represent typical conditions and may vary with water temperature, contact time, percolator geometry, and draw rate.

Mechanisms: Aqueous Diffusion = dissolution into water via gas–liquid interface; Mechanical Filtration = physical trapping of solid particulates; Condensation = vapor-phase crash-out at reduced temperature.
